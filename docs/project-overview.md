# Project Overview

## Introduction

Pricing decisions are among the most impactful business decisions in manufacturing and distribution companies. However, effective pricing requires accurate historical data, consistent data collection, and reliable analytical tools.

This project aims to develop a **Pricing Analytics Platform** that helps transform scattered operational data into actionable pricing insights.

The platform is **not** intended to automate pricing decisions or replace business expertise. Instead, it provides structured data, historical tracking, and analytical reports that support managers in making informed pricing decisions.

---

# Background

The project originated from an internal business analysis conducted for a manufacturing company using an ERP syste.

During that analysis, historical sales and pricing data were manually collected from multiple sources to measure the impact of price changes on product sales.

The resulting reports included:

* Price change percentages
* Sales changes before and after each pricing event
* Product price elasticity
* Pricing sensitivity analysis

These reports proved valuable during the company's pricing decision process, leading management to support the development of a reusable analytics platform.

---

# Problem Statement

The company currently stores pricing-related information across multiple independent sources.

Examples include:

* Current product information inside the ERP system
* Manual Excel files containing historical price changes
* Separate operational reports for sales and inventory
* Limited historical tracking for pricing data

As a result:

* Historical pricing analysis requires significant manual effort.
* Pricing reports are difficult to reproduce.
* Data collection is not standardized.
* Historical pricing information may be incomplete or unavailable.
* The analysis process depends heavily on manual work.

---

# Project Goals

The primary goals of this project are to:

* Standardize pricing-related data collection.
* Preserve historical pricing information.
* Organize pricing and sales data into a structured format.
* Automate repetitive pricing analyses.
* Generate consistent management reports.
* Reduce manual work.
* Create a maintainable system that can continue operating independently of its original developer.

---

# Project Scope

The project focuses on **pricing analytics**, not pricing automation.

The platform will support business decision-making by providing historical and analytical information such as:

* Price history
* Sales history
* Price elasticity
* Sales impact analysis
* Pricing KPIs
* Historical trends

The platform will **not** automatically determine product prices or make pricing decisions.

Management remains responsible for evaluating market conditions, competitors, strategic considerations, and final pricing decisions.

---

# Current Status

The project is currently in the **Discovery and Requirements Analysis** phase.

The current priorities include:

* Understanding the existing ERP system.
* Identifying available data sources.
* Investigating integration options.
* Defining business requirements.
* Designing an appropriate system architecture.

No implementation decisions have been finalized at this stage.

---

# Development Philosophy

This project follows an iterative engineering approach.

Rather than attempting to build a complete solution from the beginning, development is divided into incremental phases:

1. Understand the business process.
2. Discover available data.
3. Design a scalable architecture.
4. Build a Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
5. Improve automation and analytics over time.

This approach reduces technical risk while allowing the platform to evolve alongside business requirements.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision is to build a modular pricing analytics platform capable of integrating with ERP systems to provide reliable, repeatable, and scalable pricing analysis.

Potential future capabilities include:

* Automated data synchronization
* Centralized historical pricing repository
* Business dashboards
* Scenario simulation
* Pricing optimization experiments
* Internal web interface for data entry and reporting

These features will be implemented only after establishing a reliable data foundation.
