# Pricing Analytics Platform
Business analytics platform for pricing analysis, historical data management, and decision support.

---

## Overview

The Pricing Analytics Platform is a real-world software engineering and business analytics project developed to improve the pricing workflow of a manufacturing and distribution company operating in a dynamic market environment.

The company manufactures, imports, and distributes industrial power products. Because market conditions and procurement costs change frequently, pricing decisions must be reviewed on a regular basis. These decisions are currently performed through a largely manual process that relies on information scattered across multiple systems, ERP reports, spreadsheets, and manually maintained records.

The objective of this project is to design and build a centralized platform that organizes pricing-related information, preserves historical data, and provides analytical tools that support pricing decisions without replacing management's judgment.

---

## Business Problem

The existing pricing workflow presents several challenges:

- Historical pricing information is not stored in a centralized system.
- Business data is fragmented across multiple sources.
- Important reports require manual extraction from the ERP system.
- Historical analyses are difficult to reproduce.
- Decision-makers do not have all relevant information available in one place.
- The current workflow depends heavily on manual effort and individual knowledge.

This project addresses these issues by creating a standardized and reusable analytics workflow capable of organizing business data, preserving historical information, and generating consistent management reports.

---

## Development Approach

The project follows an incremental development process that begins with business discovery and requirements analysis before moving to system design and implementation.

The current focus is understanding existing business processes, identifying available data sources, investigating ERP capabilities, and defining the overall architecture.

## Guiding Principles

This project is built around several core principles:

- Business requirements before technology
- Data quality before analytics
- Documentation throughout development
- Modular and maintainable architecture
- Incremental delivery
- Repeatable analytical workflows
- Decision support rather than decision automation
